COMPAiSS

The AI that knows when not to answer.

COMPAiSS is a new class of institutional AI designed for environments where accuracy, authority, and trust matter more than speed or spectacle.

Most AI systems start by generating answers and try to control risk afterward. COMPAiSS reverses that logic.

COMPAiSS asks first:
Is AI allowed to answer this question at all?

What COMPAiSS Is

COMPAiSS is an execution-gated AI architecture built for large, regulated knowledge ecosystems such as universities, public institutions, and complex organizations.

It helps users navigate official policies, procedures, and institutional services — but only when answers can be verified against trusted, authoritative sources.

If authority cannot be established, COMPAiSS does not guess, hallucinate, or improvise.

Why COMPAiSS Is Different

Most AI systems rely on probabilities and sound confident even when wrong. COMPAiSS operates inside institution-defined truth boundaries.

This is not moderation and it is not filtering. It is architectural control.

What Problem It Solves

Organizations want AI — but not hallucinated answers, unofficial policy interpretations, or governance risk.

COMPAiSS enables AI use without surrendering institutional control. AI is allowed to operate only when it is safe, accurate, and defensible.

What COMPAiSS Is Not

COMPAiSS is not a chatbot.
It is not a FAQ bot.
It is not RAG by another name.
It is not always-on AI.

COMPAiSS is AI with permission.

AI doesn’t need to answer everything.
It needs to answer the right things, the right way.
