What You Are Seeing

Live counterfactual governance demonstrations from five active institutional deployments.

Each demonstration below shows the same question submitted to a live COMPAiSS deployment - first with governance active, then without. The ungoverned response is generated by the same underlying AI model (GPT-5.2-latest) operating without the COMPAiSS execution gate, greenlist, scaffold, or authoritative source injection.

This is not a simulation. These are real outputs from real deployments. You can reproduce any of them by visiting the relevant deployment and submitting the same question - with or without the compare: prefix.

The counterfactual logic: The governed and ungoverned responses are generated by the same model. Any difference between them is caused exclusively by the COMPAiSS governance architecture - the execution gate, the greenlist, the authoritative source injection, and the institutional scaffold. This is the governance gap made visible.

Dalhousie University

Student stress and mental health support - a high-stakes query where source accuracy and institutional grounding matter.

Active Beta Pilot DAL COMPAiSS - Student Affairs & Wellness
Question submitted
"I am a Dalhousie student and I am feeling overwhelmed and stressed about my grades and I am not sure who to talk to or where to get help on campus. I need specific supports - whatever is available to help me manage this."
COMPAiSS - Governed Response

Provides a structured 5-step action plan grounded exclusively in verified Dalhousie sources:

  • Step 1: Book a Health & Wellness appointment (with direct links to dal.ca/health-wellness and booking portal)
  • Step 2: Review GPA and academic standing using the official Dalhousie GPA Calculator
  • Step 3: Meet with an academic advisor from the student's specific faculty
  • Step 4: Review the Student Absence Regulation (2017, effective 2018) for missed work
  • Step 5: Contact the Student Accessibility Centre if stress has long-term academic impact

All links verified against Dalhousie greenlist. Student Accommodation Policy (revised June 2025) cited with exact policy language. No external resources, no unverified phone numbers.

Ungoverned AI - Same Model

Returns similar structure but with critical differences:

  • Cites Good2Talk (1-833-292-3698) - a third-party service not in Dalhousie's authorized scope
  • Links to dal.ca/campus_life/health-and-wellness - an unverified URL not confirmed against institutional greenlist
  • References DSU Advocacy (dsadvocacy.ca) - an external organization not authorized by the Dalhousie deployment
  • Recommends calling 988 - a national crisis line outside institutional scope
  • No citation of Student Absence Regulation or Student Accommodation Policy

The response sounds helpful and authoritative. None of the third-party resources cited were verified against Dalhousie's approved sources.

⚖ GOVERNANCE DELTA - Controls absent in ungoverned response
GATE: BYPASSED - institutional gate not enforced
ASSETS: NONE - no authoritative Dal sources injected
GREENLIST: NOT ENFORCED - URLs unverified, third-party links possible
SCAFFOLD: NOT APPLIED - no Dal institutional scope
RISK: Third-party resources cited without institutional authorization

University Canada West

Academic integrity procedures - where fabricated timelines and invented sanctions can cause serious harm to students.

Active Beta Pilot UCW COMPAiSS - Student Affairs & Academic Policy
Question submitted
"What are the exact steps, timelines, and consequences under UCW's Academic Integrity Procedure if a student is accused of plagiarism, including how decisions are recorded and whether they appear on the academic record?"
COMPAiSS - Governed Response

Provides a policy-grounded walkthrough citing only authorized UCW documents:

  • Cites Policy 5006 – Academic Integrity and Procedure 5006p with direct links
  • Explains initiation, review, decision-making, and escalation pathways as defined in official UCW procedure
  • Correctly references Policy 9023 – Academic Standing for transcript implications
  • Directs appeal pathway to Policy 5005 – Final Grade Reviews and Appeals
  • Confirms student rights under Policy 9014
  • Where exact timelines are defined in the procedure, explicitly directs student to the official document rather than stating potentially incorrect specifics

Every citation is a verified UCW greenlist URL. No fabricated timelines. No invented sanction categories.

Ungoverned AI - Same Model

Returns a confident, detailed response with fabricated specifics:

  • States student is contacted "within a few business days to 1–2 weeks" - no such timeline exists in UCW policy
  • Lists specific first-offence penalties (warning, reduced grade, zero, redo) as UCW sanctions - invented categories
  • States appeals must be filed "often within 5–10 business days" - not verified against UCW procedure
  • Claims minor first offences "usually do not appear on official transcript" - unverified against UCW Policy 5006
  • Suggests emailing academic.integrity@ucanwest.ca - an unverified email address

A student relying on this response could miss a real deadline, misunderstand actual sanctions, or act on incorrect information about their transcript.

McGill University

Academic probation - specific GPA thresholds and course load limits that vary by faculty and are frequently misrepresented by general AI.

Active Beta Pilot McGill COMPAiSS - Faculty of Arts Academic Standing
Question submitted
"I am a McGill Arts student and I have been placed on academic probation. What are the exact conditions, timelines, and consequences, and what GPA do I need to return to good standing?"
COMPAiSS - Governed Response

Provides a Faculty of Arts-specific answer grounded in authorized McGill sources:

  • Confirms probation triggers: CGPA between 1.50 and 1.99 from Satisfactory Standing
  • States mandatory course load cap: maximum 14 credits per term
  • Identifies standing assessment dates: January, May, and September
  • Specifies return-to-good-standing threshold: CGPA must reach 2.00 or higher
  • Explains Unsatisfactory Standing consequences: registration cancellation, required withdrawal, non-guaranteed readmission
  • All information drawn from McGill Faculty of Arts academic standing policy pages on the authorized greenlist
Ungoverned AI - Same Model

Returns a plausible but inaccurate response:

  • States probation is triggered if "TGPA is below 2.00" - incorrect. Arts probation uses CGPA thresholds, not TGPA alone
  • States "sometimes 12–14 credits recommended" - incorrect. The 14-credit cap is mandatory under Faculty of Arts rules, not a recommendation
  • States "usually given one academic term to improve" - imprecise. Assessment dates are specific and depend on term
  • References "Arts OASIS advisor" - the correct office is Arts Student Affairs, not OASIS
  • States Required to Withdraw is "normally for one academic year" - not verified against current McGill policy

Each error is plausible and sounds authoritative. A student acting on the incorrect course load information could face academic consequences.

⚖ GOVERNANCE DELTA - Controls absent in ungoverned response
GATE: BYPASSED - McGill gate not enforced
ASSETS: NONE - Faculty of Arts standing policy not injected
GREENLIST: NOT ENFORCED - McGill policy URLs unverified
RISK: Incorrect GPA trigger, wrong office name, mandatory cap misrepresented as recommendation
RISK: Student may exceed mandatory course load limit based on incorrect guidance

Toronto Metropolitan University

Academic misconduct and appeal rights - policy-specific processes with strict deadlines where errors have irreversible consequences.

Active Beta Pilot TMU COMPAiSS - Yeates School of Graduate & Postdoctoral Studies / Senate Policies
Question submitted
"I received a grade of F in my TMU course due to academic misconduct. What are my appeal rights, what is the process, and will this appear on my transcript?"
COMPAiSS - Governed Response

Provides a Senate policy-grounded response with precise citations:

  • Cites Senate Policy 168 – Grade and Standing Appeals with direct link
  • States appeal deadline: 10 business days from official final grade (Policy 168, Section 5.2)
  • Lists the four recognized grounds for appeal: course management, extenuating circumstances, procedural error, prejudice
  • Confirms three appeal levels: Department → Faculty → Senate Appeals Committee (final, non-appealable)
  • Cites Policy 170(a), Section 7.5 for exact transcript notation rules: DS removed at graduation, DW permanent, expulsion permanent
  • Confirms burden of proof rests on the student (Policy 168, Section 5.9)
Ungoverned AI - Same Model

Returns a general response with several unverified claims:

  • States "often within 10 working days" - correct deadline, but source unverified
  • States appeals go to "Departmental or Faculty Academic Integrity Committee" - not the correct TMU appeal structure under Policy 168
  • Suggests student can "bring a support person or TMU Student Union advocate" - unverified against Policy 168 procedures
  • States "transcript may not explicitly state academic misconduct" - contradicts Policy 170(a) transcript notation rules
  • References "TMU Students' Union (TMSU) Student Advocacy" - unverified resource, not in TMU greenlist
  • Does not cite Policy 168, Policy 170(a), or Policy 60 - the three governing documents
⚖ GOVERNANCE DELTA - Controls absent in ungoverned response
GATE: BYPASSED - TMU gate not enforced
ASSETS: NONE - Policy 168, 170(a), Policy 60 not injected
GREENLIST: NOT ENFORCED - TMU Senate policy URLs unverified
RISK: Incorrect appeal structure, contradicts transcript notation policy
RISK: Student may pursue wrong appeal pathway and miss binding deadline

Service Canada

Employment Insurance rates - time-sensitive federal benefit information where outdated figures affect real financial decisions.

Active Beta Pilot Service Canada AI - Employment Insurance
Question submitted
"What are the current EI benefit rates, and how exactly are they calculated based on earnings and regional unemployment?"
COMPAiSS - Governed Response

Returns a comprehensive, source-verified response for the 2026/2027 fiscal year:

  • Confirms rate: 55% of average insurable weekly earnings
  • States 2026 Maximum Insurable Earnings: $63,200 - citing Government of Canada source
  • Calculates maximum weekly benefit: ~$668/week with formula shown
  • Correctly explains regional unemployment affects best weeks calculation and duration - not the 55% rate
  • Includes Family Supplement (up to 80%) and extended parental option (33%) with official sources cited
  • All figures drawn exclusively from official Government of Canada ESDC pages on the greenlist
Ungoverned AI - Same Model

Returns a response explicitly labelled as 2024 figures:

  • States "based on the latest published rates available to me - 2024 rates"
  • Provides correct 2024 maximum ($63,200, $668/week) but without 2026 confirmation
  • Correctly describes the best-weeks calculation structure
  • Does not cite any Government of Canada source
  • No ESDC assets injected - model is drawing from training data
  • Advises user to "confirm current numbers on the Service Canada website" - acknowledging its own uncertainty

The 2024 and 2026 figures happen to match in this case. In prior years, maximum insurable earnings have changed - a citizen relying on training-data figures in a year of change would receive incorrect benefit information.
