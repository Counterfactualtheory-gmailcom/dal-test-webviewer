McGill University

Academic probation - specific GPA thresholds and course load limits that vary by faculty and are frequently misrepresented by general AI.

Active Beta Pilot McGill COMPAiSS - Faculty of Arts Academic Standing
Question submitted
"I am a McGill Arts student and I have been placed on academic probation. What are the exact conditions, timelines, and consequences, and what GPA do I need to return to good standing?"
COMPAiSS - Governed Response

I'm really glad you reached out. Being placed on academic probation (Probationary Standing) can feel stressful - but it also means you are still allowed to continue in your program, under specific conditions. Below is a clear, step-by-step breakdown tailored to you as a Faculty of Arts undergraduate student for the 2025-2026 academic year.

1. Why You Were Placed on Probation (Faculty of Arts)

In the Faculty of Arts, Academic Standing is based primarily on your Cumulative GPA (CGPA) and sometimes also your Term GPA (TGPA). You are placed in Probationary Standing if your CGPA falls between 1.50 and 1.99, and you were previously in Satisfactory Standing.

2. Exact Conditions While on Probation

A. Reduced Course Load: You must carry a maximum of 14 credits per term. This is mandatory - not optional.

B. Academic Improvement Requirement: You must raise your CGPA to 2.00 or higher to return to Satisfactory (good) Standing.

C. Advising Requirement: You are expected to consult your departmental advisor about course selection and meet with your Faculty advisor (Arts Student Affairs) to discuss degree planning.

3. Timeline: When Standing Is Assessed

Academic Standing is assessed in January (for Fall term - interim decision), May (after Winter term - final decision for the academic year), and September (if Summer courses were taken). The most important standing decision is made after the Winter term.

4. What GPA Do You Need to Return to Good Standing?

To return to Satisfactory Standing, you must raise your Cumulative GPA (CGPA) to 2.00 or higher. It is not enough to just have one good term - your overall CGPA must reach 2.00.

5. Consequences If You Do Not Improve

You will be placed in Unsatisfactory Standing if your CGPA falls below 1.50, or your TGPA is below 2.50 and your CGPA is below 2.00 after already being on probation. If placed in Unsatisfactory Standing: you may not continue in your program, your registration will be cancelled, you must apply for readmission, and readmission is not automatic or guaranteed. If you are in Unsatisfactory Standing for a second time, you must withdraw permanently.

Toronto Metropolitan University

Academic misconduct and appeal rights - policy-specific processes with strict deadlines where errors have irreversible consequences.

Active Beta Pilot TMU COMPAiSS - Yeates School of Graduate & Postdoctoral Studies / Senate Policies
Question submitted
"I received a grade of F in my TMU course due to academic misconduct. What are my appeal rights, what is the process, and will this appear on my transcript?"
COMPAiSS - Governed Response

I'm sorry you're going through this — receiving an F due to academic misconduct is stressful and can feel overwhelming. All guidance below is based strictly on current TMU Senate policies for the 2025/2026 academic year.

1️⃣ Your Right to Appeal

Your appeal rights are governed by: Senate Policy 168 – Grade and Standing Appeals: Grade And Standing Appeals Policy 168

If you received a final grade of F as a result of academic misconduct, you may appeal the final grade. If your academic standing changed (e.g., Required to Withdraw), you may also appeal the standing. You must first attempt informal resolution before submitting a formal appeal (Policy 168, Section 5.2–5.4).

2️⃣ Important Distinction: Academic Misconduct & Appeals

Academic misconduct cases are governed by Senate Policy 60 – Academic Integrity (Referenced in Policy 168 and Policy 170(a)). If the F was assigned following a misconduct finding: the academic integrity decision itself is handled under Policy 60; a grade appeal under Policy 168 does not replace the misconduct process; you may appeal if proper grounds exist.

3️⃣ Grounds Required to Appeal

Under Policy 168 (Section 5.6 and Section 6), you may appeal only if one or more of the following grounds exist:

  • ✅ Course Management — a significant deviation from the course outline, course management policies, or fair grading procedures
  • ✅ Extenuating Circumstances — events outside your control that could not be reasonably foreseen and significantly affected your academic performance (examples include health crises, compassionate grounds, traumatic events, legal commitments)
  • ✅ Procedural Error — an error in how University policy was applied that affected your grade or academic standing
  • ✅ Prejudice — if your grade was impacted by discrimination based on protected grounds under the Ontario Human Rights Code (appeal automatically forwarded to Human Rights Services)

4️⃣ Appeal Process – Step by Step

🔹 Level 1: Department/School/Program
⏰ Deadline: submit within 10 business days of receiving your official final grade or standing. Decision maker responds in writing within 10 business days (14 for MD).

🔹 Level 2: Faculty
⏰ Deadline: 10 business days after receiving the Level 1 decision.

🔹 Level 3: Senate Appeals Committee (SAC)
Reviews submissions, may dismiss if grounds are not met, may hold a hearing, issues a final non-appealable decision. Senate-level decisions are final (Policy 168, Section 3.10).

5️⃣ Burden of Proof

Under Policy 168, Section 5.9: the burden of proof is on the student. You must show, on a balance of probabilities, that the final grade is incorrect or that the academic standing decision should be changed. Substantial documentation is required (Policy 168, Section 5.10).

6️⃣ Will This Appear on Your Transcript?

Under: Policy 170(a): Undergraduate Course Grading, Academic Program Standing, and Eligibility to Graduate: Undergraduate Course Grading Academic Program Standing And Eligibility To Graduate Policy 170 A

  • ✅ An F grade appears on your official transcript and is calculated in your GPA (0.00 grade points)
  • ✅ If a DEF (Deferred) was temporarily assigned: it is replaced by a final grade once the misconduct case is resolved. Policy 170(a), Section 5.5.4 confirms: DEF is replaced by a final course grade upon resolution under Policy 60.
  • ✅ Policy 170(a), Section 7.5: Disciplinary Suspension (DS) appears on transcript but is removed upon graduation. Disciplinary Withdrawal (DW) appears permanently. Expulsion appears permanently.

If only an F grade was assigned (without disciplinary standing), the transcript shows the F like any other failed course.

7️⃣ Can the Grade Be Changed?

Policy 168, Section 7: if an appeal is granted, the decision maker may deny the appeal, grant the appeal, or grant or deny in part, with conditions. If you accept a remedy that results in a grade change, that outcome cannot be further appealed (Policy 168, Section 5.15).

8️⃣ Critical Timeline Warning

📌 You have 10 business days from receiving your official final grade to file a Level 1 appeal. Missing this deadline can eliminate your appeal rights unless special circumstances are accepted.

🔎 Summary of Key Policies

  • Policy 168 – Grade and Standing Appeals: Grade And Standing Appeals Policy 168
  • Policy 170(a) – Undergraduate Course Grading: Undergraduate Course Grading Academic Program Standing And Eligibility To Graduate Policy 170 A
  • Policy 60 – Academic Integrity (referenced throughout appeal and DEF processes)

Employment Insurance rates - time-sensitive federal benefit information where outdated figures affect real financial decisions.

Active Beta Pilot Service Canada AI - Employment Insurance
Question submitted
"What are the current EI benefit rates, and how exactly are they calculated based on earnings and regional unemployment?"
COMPAiSS - Governed Response

Below is a complete, structured explanation of the current EI benefit rate formula, how weekly amounts are calculated, the 2026 maximum insurable earnings, how regional unemployment affects duration (not rate), special rules (Family Supplement, special benefits), step-by-step calculation examples, and planning considerations. All information is based exclusively on official Government of Canada sources.

1️⃣ The Core EI Benefit Rate (Set by Law)

Under the Employment Insurance Act, regular EI benefits are calculated at 55% of your average insurable weekly earnings, up to a maximum amount.
(Source: Government of Canada — EI maternity and parental benefits: Benefit amount)
This 55% rate is fixed in legislation and does not change by region.

2️⃣ Maximum Insurable Earnings — 2026

For 2026, the Government of Canada has set:
✅ Maximum Insurable Earnings: $63,200
(Source: Government of Canada — 2026 Maximum Insurable Earnings)
EI only insures earnings up to $63,200 per year. Even if you earn more, EI calculations stop at that cap.

📌 Maximum Weekly Benefit:
$63,200 ÷ 52 weeks = $1,215.38/week
55% of $1,215.38 = $668.46/week
✅ Maximum weekly EI benefit in 2026: approximately $668

3️⃣ How EI Calculates Your Weekly Payment

EI uses your best weeks of earnings. The Government of Canada explains: "We calculate your benefit amount using your best (highest paid) weeks of earnings during your qualifying period."
(Source: EI maternity and parental benefits: Benefit amount)

Step 1 — Identify your qualifying period: usually the 52 weeks before your claim starts.
Step 2 — Determine number of "best weeks" based on your region's unemployment rate (higher unemployment → fewer weeks required).
Step 3 — Average those best weeks: add up insurable earnings from required best weeks, divide by number of weeks used.
Step 4 — Apply 55%: multiply average weekly earnings × 55%.
Step 5 — Apply maximum cap: if result exceeds ~$668/week, your payment is capped.

4️⃣ Example Calculations

Example 1 — Worker earning $1,000/week:
55% of $1,000 = $550/week ✅ Weekly EI = $550

Example 2 — Worker earning $1,500/week:
Maximum insurable weekly earnings = ~$1,215
55% of that = ~$668 ✅ Weekly EI = $668

5️⃣ How Regional Unemployment Affects EI

Regional unemployment does NOT change the 55% rate. It affects:

  • ✅ A. Number of hours required to qualify — higher unemployment → fewer hours required
  • ✅ B. Number of best weeks used — higher unemployment → fewer weeks averaged → may increase benefit
  • ✅ C. Duration of benefits — higher unemployment → more weeks payable

📌 Many people believe unemployment rate changes the percentage paid. It does not. The rate is always 55%, except in special cases below.

6️⃣ Family Supplement (Low-Income Families)

If you have children, receive the Canada Child Benefit, and have low family income, you may receive a Family Supplement which can increase your benefit rate to up to 80% of your average insurable earnings.
(Source: EI maternity and parental benefits: Benefit amount)
This applies automatically if CRA information confirms eligibility.

Voici un resume structure de la procedure d'integrite academique de University Canada West (UCW). Les details peuvent etre mis a jour ; il faut toujours verifier la version la plus recente du Academic Calendar ou du site officiel de UCW.

1. Etapes exactes de la procedure en cas d'accusation de plagiat

Etape 1 : Detection et signalement - Le professeur detecte un soupcon de plagiat, rassemble les preuves et signale le cas formellement a l'administration academique.

Etape 2 : Notification officielle a l'etudiant - L'etudiant recoit un avis ecrit officiel par email UCW incluant la description de l'infraction presumee, les preuves, la politique applicable, les sanctions possibles et le delai pour repondre.

Etape 3 : Evaluation et decision - Pour les cas simples, le professeur et/ou le Dean peuvent rendre une decision sans audience formelle. Pour les cas graves ou recidives, le dossier est transmis a un Academic Integrity Committee.

Etape 4 : Decision officielle - L'etudiant recoit une decision ecrite, la sanction imposee, les modalites d'appel et le delai pour faire appel.

2. Sanctions prevues (progressives)

Premiere infraction mineure : avertissement ecrit, note zero sur l'assignation, obligation de suivre un module sur l'integrite academique.

Infraction moderee : note zero sur l'assignation, echec du cours (F), mention disciplinaire interne.

Infraction grave ou recidive : suspension temporaire, expulsion, mention disciplinaire permanente.

4. Comment la decision est-elle enregistree ?

Toutes les infractions sont enregistrees dans le dossier academique interne. Si note zero sur un devoir - seule la note finale du cours apparait, aucune mention de plagiat. UCW applique une politique de tolerance stricte en cas de recidive. Les etudiants internationaux doivent etre particulierement prudents : une suspension peut affecter le statut d'immigration.
